## The Business Scenario (Fictional Case)
Over the last period, we ran massive digital campaigns across multiple platforms to drive our e-commerce sales. The goal of this report is to see what's working, what's not, and where we should put our money next.

### The Numbers That Actually Matter (Key Insights)

1. **The ROI is off the charts (915.86%):** We spent $11 million on ads, and we brought in $113 million in total sales. For every dollar we put into the machine, we got over $9 back. This tells me our customer acquisition cost (CAC) is incredibly healthy right now.

2. **Meta and Google are carrying the team:** Looking at the platform split, Meta Ads (43.49%) and Google Ads (27.71%) are eating up most of our budget, but based on the overall revenue, they are clearly driving the bulk of the conversions. 

3. **We have unexpected goldmines:** If you look at the Top 5 Countries, the US or Western Europe aren't leading. The *Nepal* is our top buyer, closely followed by Bolivia and Argentina. We are getting massive traction in emerging markets.

### What should we do next? (Action Items)

If I were leading the data strategy for this company, my immediate recommendations would be:

* **Open the wallet for Meta Ads:** With a 900% plus ROI, we are leaving money on the table. We should evaluate to aggressively scale our ad spend on Meta and Google until we hit diminishing returns.
* **Localize everything for LATAM:** Since Bolivia and Argentina are in our Top 3, we need to double down there. We should tailor our ad copy to local slang, ensure we accept local payment methods, and optimize shipping routes to South America.
* **Investigate LinkedIn Ads:** It's taking up nearly 16% of our budget. If we are selling B2B high-ticket items, great. But if we are selling regular consumer goods, LinkedIn is way too expensive, and we should move that money over to Google.
